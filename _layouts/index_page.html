Turbulence Structure Laboratory

Introduction

Turbulence Structure Laboratory is a research laboratory, affiliated with the School of Mechanical Engineering, Faculty of Engineering and Tel Aviv University. We study the structure and dynamics of turbulent flows.
Turbulence it is a disordered motion of fluid that has enormous practical importance. We believe that detailed experiments, jointly performed with the numerical simulations and leaded by the theoretical predictions will help us gaining better insight into the structure of turbulence.
We develop novel measurement techniques, both hardware and software, all devoted to turbulence research. For example, we design and develop miniature multi-hot-cold film anemometers, three-dimensional particle tracking velocimetry with real-time image processing, and flexible blade rheometers

Open positions

If you want to develop next generations of experimental methods for fluid dynamics or heat transfer; if you want to understand better the physics of turbulent flows, contact us. We are looking for excellent students and top researchers.

Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD)

We develop data mining and numerical method tools that help understand better the underlying structure of turbulent flows in experimental and numerical data.

In-filed experiment, Swiss Alps

Turbulent velocity, velocity and scalar gradients measurements in the fully developed atmospheric boundary layer at very high Reynolds number using the 25-wires hot-cold-wire probe.

3D-PTV

We develop experimental methods of Particle Image Velocimetry, three-dimensional Particle Tracking Velocimetry, object tracking in visible and IR spectrum and implement these in the studies of turbulent structure, particles/bubbles/droplets motion in turbulence, complex bio-medical and bio-physical flows and animals-turbulence interaction.

Highlights
